# Ethics Exemption Determination

**Project:** Australian-Weighted Bayesian Returned-Traveller Fever Differential with Live Outbreak Priors
**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Determined by:** Hayden Farquhar

---

## Data sources used in this study

1. **GeoSentinel surveillance publications** — aggregate, published destination-diagnosis frequency tables from peer-reviewed literature. No individual patient data.
2. **Australian NNDSS public datasets** — aggregate annual notification counts by condition, publicly released by the Australian Government Department of Health and Aged Care. No individual patient data.
3. **Published case series** — individual case-level data extracted from published peer-reviewed manuscripts. All data are already in the public domain via journal publication. No contact with patients or treating clinicians.
4. **ProMED-mail and WHO Disease Outbreak News** — publicly available outbreak alert feeds. No patient-level data.
5. **CDC Yellow Book** — publicly available clinical reference material.

## Determination

This study does not involve:
- Collection of new data from human participants
- Access to identifiable patient records
- Linkage of datasets that could re-identify individuals
- Intervention or interaction with any person
- Access to restricted or controlled health data

All data sources are either aggregate published statistics or individual case data already published in peer-reviewed literature. The study constitutes secondary analysis of publicly available data.

Under the Australian National Statement on Ethical Conduct in Human Research (2023 update), Section 5.1.22, research that uses existing collections of data or records that contain only non-identifiable data is exempt from HREC review.

**Classification:** HREC-exempt.

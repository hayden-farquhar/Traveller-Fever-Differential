# Australian Imported-Case Data Sources Inventory

**Compiled:** 2026-04-18
**Purpose:** Sources for Australian reweighting of GeoSentinel destination priors

---

## Tier 1: High-value published papers with destination-level imported-case data

### Malaria — Sohail et al. 2024
- **Title:** "Imported malaria into Australia: surveillance insights and opportunities"
- *J Travel Med* 31(3) | PMID: 38127641 | DOI: 10.1093/jtm/taad164 | PMC: PMC10998534
- **Years:** 2012-2022
- **Cases:** 3,204 (all imported — no local transmission since 1981)
- **Species:** P. falciparum 1,840 (59%), P. vivax 996 (32%), P. ovale 167 (5%), P. malariae 98 (3%), P. knowlesi 2 (<1%)
- **Region of acquisition:** Sub-Saharan Africa 1,433 (45%), Oceania 569 (18%), Southern/Central Asia 367 (12%), North Africa/Middle East 348 (11%), SE Asia 194 (6%)
- **Top countries:** PNG (474), India (277)
- **Incidence per traveller:** 1.34-7.71/100,000 trips (2012-2019)
- **USEFULNESS: HIGH** — primary malaria prior source with species + destination stratification

### Dengue — Sohail et al. 2024
- **Title:** "The epidemiology of imported and locally acquired dengue in Australia, 2012-2022"
- *J Travel Med* 31(2) | PMID: 38243558 | DOI: 10.1093/jtm/taae014 | PMC: PMC10911064
- **Years:** 2012-2022
- **Cases:** 13,343 total; 12,568 imported (94.2%), 584 locally acquired (4.4%)
- **Region (imported):** SE Asia 9,323 (74%), Southern/Central Asia 1,555 (12%), Oceania 1,341 (11%)
- **Top countries:** Indonesia 5,778 (46%), Thailand 1,483 (12%), India 803 (6%), Philippines 550 (4%), Malaysia 435 (3%), Sri Lanka 426 (3%), Fiji 370 (3%), Vietnam 297 (2%), PNG 280 (2%), Timor-Leste 246 (2%)
- **Serotypes:** DENV-2 42%, DENV-1 30%, DENV-3 8%, DENV-4 3%
- **Incidence per traveller:** 8.29-22.10/100,000 movements (2012-2019); 34.79/100,000 in 2022
- **USEFULNESS: HIGH** — primary dengue prior source with detailed destination data

### Typhoid — Forster & Leder 2021
- **Title:** "Typhoid fever in travellers: estimating the risk of acquisition by country"
- *J Travel Med* 28(8) | PMID: 34619766 | DOI: 10.1093/jtm/taab150 | PMC: PMC8715417
- **Years:** 2010-2017
- **Cases:** 923 (96% overseas-acquired)
- **Region:** South Asia highest (252/100,000 PY), particularly Bangladesh
- **Country-level:** Yes — incidence rates per 100,000 PY by country
- **USEFULNESS: HIGH** — per-destination typhoid risk denominators for Australian travellers

## Tier 2: Useful for specific diseases or time periods

### NAMAC Annual Reports (arboviruses + malaria, 2004-2015)
- **Location:** https://ojs.cdi.cdc.gov.au/ (NAMAC category)
- **Years:** 2004-05 through 2014-15 (most recent published)
- **Diseases:** Dengue, chikungunya, Zika, Japanese encephalitis, malaria, Ross River, Barmah Forest, Murray Valley encephalitis
- **Imported breakdown:** YES with country-of-acquisition tables
- **Example (2013-14):** Dengue 1,795 overseas-acquired; Chikungunya 94 (all imported, top: Indonesia 47, India 10); Malaria 373 (India 56, Sudan 43, PNG 34); JE 2 (Philippines, Taiwan)
- **USEFULNESS: HIGH for 2004-2015** — gap from 2015-16 onward filled by Sohail papers

### Chikungunya time-series — Furuya-Kanamori et al. 2019
- *PLOS NTDs* | PMID: 31107863
- **Years:** 2008-2017 NNDSS data
- **Data:** Monthly imported counts with country-of-acquisition; geographical expansion over time
- **USEFULNESS: MODERATE** — temporal chikungunya trend with destinations

### JE emergence — Reyes Veliz et al. 2025
- *CDI* | PMID: 39837615 | DOI: 10.33321/cdi.2025.49.005
- **Years:** 2021-2022
- **Data:** 42 confirmed/probable locally-acquired JEV cases (2022 Australian outbreak); 7 deaths (CFR 17%)
- **Context:** Prior to 2021, only 6 locally-acquired human JE cases ever in Australia. Changes JE from purely-imported to potentially endemic.
- **USEFULNESS: MODERATE** — critical for JE prior calibration

### VFR enhanced surveillance — Heywood et al. 2016
- *Epidemiol Infect* 144(16):3554-3563 | PMID: 27574034 | DOI: 10.1017/S0950268816001734 | PMC: PMC5111124
- **Years:** Feb 2013 to Jan 2014 (1 year, NSW + VIC)
- **Diseases:** Typhoid, paratyphoid, measles, hep A, hep E, malaria, chikungunya
- **Cases:** 222 enhanced surveillance; 180 travel-associated; 117 VFR
- **USEFULNESS: MODERATE** — small but individual-level multi-disease Australian data

### TB surveillance — Bright et al. 2020
- *CDI* | DOI: 10.33321/cdi.2020.44.55
- **Years:** 2015-2018
- **Data:** Overseas-born 86-89% of TB notifications; incidence 18-20/100,000 in overseas-born vs ~1/100,000 in Australian-born
- **Limitation:** Uses "overseas-born" not "recently imported" — TB reactivation complicates use
- **USEFULNESS: MODERATE** — context for TB priors but not directly travel-associated

### Aggregate NNDSS overview — Sohail et al. 2024
- *BMC Global and Public Health* 2(1):1 | PMID: 39681889 | DOI: 10.1186/s44263-023-00029-y | PMC: PMC11622879
- **Years:** 2012-2022
- **Data:** All 68 notifiable diseases; 23,622 "imported disease" notifications total; COVID impact analysis
- **Limitation:** Does NOT provide per-disease imported counts or country-of-acquisition
- **USEFULNESS: LOW** — envelope only

## Tier 3: CDI NNDSS Annual Reports (aggregate, 1994-2016)

- **Location:** https://ojs.cdi.cdc.gov.au/ (NNDSS_annual_reports category)
- **Most recent:** 2016 data year (published May 2021, DOI: 10.33321/cdi.2021.45.28)
- **Coverage:** All ~68 notifiable diseases
- **Imported breakdown:** Available for "travel-associated" diseases but completeness of country-of-acquisition ~24%
- **Gap:** No annual report published for 2017 onward

## Recommended approach for P59

1. **Use Tier 1 papers** (malaria, dengue, typhoid) as primary NNDSS substitutes — they provide exactly what we need
2. **Extract NAMAC reports** for chikungunya, JE, and historical malaria/dengue data (2004-2015)
3. **Submit NNDSS data request** for: hep A, hep E, measles, leptospirosis (2012-2024) with place-of-acquisition
4. **Locally-acquired diseases** (Q fever, melioidosis, rickettsial infections, leptospirosis): set low but non-zero imported priors based on NNDSS annual report counts; note that these are predominantly domestic diagnoses
5. **TB:** Use cautiously — overseas-born ≠ recently returned traveller
6. **COVID-19:** Include in model but with ubiquitous prior (no destination weighting)
